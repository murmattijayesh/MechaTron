## Overview

A CubeSat is a miniaturised satellite built to a standardised 10×10×10 cm unit for cost-effective deployment in low Earth orbit. Because launch and orbital conditions are brutal, the structure has to be proven on paper long before it flies. I modelled the CubeSat frame in SolidWorks and ran a complete finite element campaign in Ansys to verify its structural integrity across static, dynamic and stability load cases.

## Objective

Confirm that the frame survives launch loads and the orbital environment — with adequate safety margins, controlled deformation, no resonance during launch, and strong resistance to compressive buckling.

## Approach

- **Material:** Aluminium 7075-T6 — E = 71.7 GPa, ν = 0.33, ρ = 2810 kg/m³, S<sub>ut</sub> = 572 MPa, S<sub>yt</sub> = 503 MPa.
- **Static structural** analysis to evaluate stress, deformation and factor of safety under static loading.
- **Modal** analysis to extract natural frequencies and check for resonance against the launch environment.
- **Eigenvalue buckling** analysis to quantify resistance to compressive instability.
- **Tools:** SolidWorks (CAD) and Ansys (FEA).

## Results

- **Safety factor: 5.44 to 15** across the structure, confirming structural integrity under static loads.
- **Maximum total deformation: 0.27 mm (2.69 × 10⁻⁴ m)** — minimal displacement, ensuring structural stability in orbit.
- **First-mode natural frequency: 7.92 Hz**, within the safe range and clear of launch excitation, preventing resonance.
- **Buckling load multiplier: 158.96**, demonstrating strong compressive resistance.

## Outcome

The analysis validated the CubeSat frame as launch-ready, with quantified margins on strength, stiffness, vibration and buckling. The workflow — CAD in SolidWorks, multi-physics validation in Ansys — is directly transferable to qualifying any lightweight aerospace structure before manufacture.
