## Overview

1D analysis of planar trusses simplifies structural evaluation by examining axial forces in the members, making it an efficient, fast and cost-effective way to verify a design. I built a line-body (1D) model of a planar truss and ran a static structural analysis in Ansys to resolve member forces, deformation and stress.

## Why 1D analysis

- **Efficiency:** reduces complex structural behaviour to tractable axial-force calculations.
- **Design & safety:** confirms the structure is safe and efficient under load.
- **Cost-effectiveness:** optimises material usage to reduce cost.

## Approach

- **Material:** Douglas Fir — E = 13.1 GPa, ν = 0.29.
- **Loading:** 90 kN applied load with appropriate supports.
- **Tools:** SolidWorks (geometry) and Ansys (1D static structural FEA).

## Results

- **Maximum total deformation: 6.07 mm** — minimal on the true scale, confirming the design is safe.
- **Direct stress:** +8.19 MPa maximum (tensile) and −17.45 MPa minimum (compressive), clearly separating members in tension from those in compression.
- Identified the critical members, verified stability, and highlighted clear optimisation potential in lightly loaded members.

## Outcome

The analysis delivered a clear picture of force distribution and the load path through the truss, pinpointing the members that govern the design. It is a reusable methodology for quickly screening and optimising frame structures such as bridges, towers, cranes and aerospace/automotive frames.
