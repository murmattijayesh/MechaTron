## Overview

Early detection is critical for treating life-threatening brain tumours, and automated assistance can reduce radiologists' workload and human error. I built a supervised machine-learning pipeline that classifies MRI scans for the presence of a tumour and reports its size, delivered through a simple MATLAB GUI.

## Methodology

- **Data preparation:** collected and labelled MRI images ("yes" for tumour, "no" for non-tumour) and standardised them for model input.
- **Model training:** trained a supervised classifier on the labelled dataset.
- **Detection:** identified and marked tumours in new scans, computing the tumour size as a percentage of the image.
- **GUI development:** built a MATLAB GUI to upload an image and run detection, presenting the result clearly.

## Results

- **High validation accuracy**, reliably distinguishing tumour from non-tumour scans.
- **Rapid, consistent analysis** compared with manual inspection — acting as a useful second opinion for radiologists.
- **Quantitative output** such as tumour-size percentage, through an easy-to-use interface accessible to non-technical users.

## Outcome

The project demonstrates a complete applied-ML workflow — from data labelling and model training to a usable, GUI-driven tool — applied to a high-impact biomedical problem.
