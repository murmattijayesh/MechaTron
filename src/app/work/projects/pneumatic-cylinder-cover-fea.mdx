## Overview

This project covers the finite element analysis of a pneumatic cylinder cover to ensure its structural integrity under operating conditions. The goal was to evaluate stress distribution, deformation and factor of safety, and confirm the design's suitability for real-world use.

## Approach

- **Material:** engineering plastic — E = 22 GPa, ν = 0.3.
- **Loading:** 0.5 MPa operating pressure with representative constraints.
- **Tools:** SolidWorks (CAD) and Ansys (static structural FEA).

## Results

- **Equivalent (von-Mises) stress:** 13.21 MPa maximum, within the material's limits.
- **Equivalent elastic strain:** 6.08 × 10⁻³ maximum.
- **Total deformation:** 9.22 × 10⁻³ mm maximum — minimal and acceptable under the operating load.

## Findings

The analysis identified the critical, highest-stress regions and confirmed they stay within material limits, while deformation remained negligible. The computed factor of safety met the design requirements, validating the cylinder cover as structurally sound for industrial machinery, automotive, aerospace, robotics and manufacturing applications.
