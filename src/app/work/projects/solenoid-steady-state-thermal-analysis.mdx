## Overview

A solenoid's performance and lifespan depend on managing the heat it generates — excessive temperature degrades performance and can cause failure. I ran a steady-state thermal analysis of a multi-material solenoid assembly in Ansys to map the temperature distribution, identify hotspots and verify the design operates safely.

## Approach

- **Materials:** grey cast iron core, copper-alloy coil, structural-steel bracket and polyethylene insulator.
- **Boundary conditions:** internal heat generation of 1 × 10⁶ W/m³ in the coil, convection at 50 W/m²·°C with a 25 °C ambient.
- **Tools:** SolidWorks (CAD) and Ansys (steady-state thermal FEA).

## Results

- **Peak temperature: 67.2 °C** (from a 25 °C ambient) — within the safe operating limits of all materials.
- **Directional heat flux:** 5.40 × 10⁵ W/m² maximum.
- Hotspots were located near regions of high current density and limited heat dissipation.

## Findings & Recommendations

The temperature field highlighted exactly where the assembly runs hottest. Based on the results I recommended enhanced cooling, reduced current density, and higher-thermal-conductivity materials in the critical regions. The simulation results aligned with expected thermal behaviour, validating the model for further design iterations.
