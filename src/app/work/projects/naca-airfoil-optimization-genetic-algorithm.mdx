## Overview

The goal was to optimise the profile of a NACA 4-digit series airfoil to maximise lift, using a genetic algorithm to search the design space and CFD to validate the winner. The project pairs a machine-learning optimisation loop with physics-based simulation.

## Approach

- **Optimiser:** developed and implemented a genetic algorithm in MATLAB to evolve the airfoil shape (camber, position and thickness) toward maximum lift.
- **Validation:** ran CFD simulations in Ansys Fluent on the optimised profile, with appropriate boundary conditions and turbulence models for accurate results.
- **Iteration:** repeatedly adjusted airfoil parameters in the genetic algorithm to converge on an optimal design, benchmarked against a baseline NACA 2414.

## Results

- **200% increase in lift** versus the baseline airfoil, confirming substantially improved aerodynamic performance.
- The optimised shape was validated through detailed CFD analysis, ensuring the result was reliable rather than a numerical artefact of the optimiser.
- Demonstrated a clear, repeatable methodology for future airfoil optimisation combining genetic algorithms with CFD.

## Outcome

The project shows an end-to-end, data-driven design loop: an ML optimiser proposes shapes, CFD verifies them, and the two iterate to a validated, high-performance result — a workflow that generalises well beyond airfoils to other shape-optimisation problems.
